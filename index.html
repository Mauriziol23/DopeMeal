Made with Love

While working at the headquarters of a major grocer, I received a sample of overnight oats that I thought my son would like. So, like any person who likes free stuff, I brought the sample home, and as my child read the package he frowned at the flavor and asked if we could make our own. I shrugged and said “sure!” We gathered our ingredients and then got to work. Smelling excitedly along the way, we then put the oats in the fridge and went on with our day. The next morning, he opened the container and said “wow Mom, this smells so good! He excitedly took a bite, wiggled his head and then smiled. He said “Mom, I think we’re on to something” and asked if he could save some for his best friend; once again I agreed. After his friend tried our oats, he bounced back and forth and said “Ms. Darian, this is so good, you could sell it!” That was the start of Oatmeal & Company…

Oatmeal & Company is not your basic oatmeal. The “& Company” represents the combination of organic rolled oats and the amazing flavors that we’ve thoughtful paired together. Made in my kitchen with an immense amount of love and pride. It is my sincerest hope that you’ll enjoy every bite. Many Thanks,


Darian, Owner of Oatmeal & Company

Explore our Flavors

Blueberry Cardamom: Blueberry and Cardamom pair well together to offer a great flavor profile that is rounded out with middle notes of Madagascar vanilla. The fragrantly flavorful cardamom is surely the star of this delicious blend.

The Softest Oatmeal Cookies Ever!
Ingredients:
  • 1 cup butter, softened
  • 1 cup white sugar
  • 1 cup packed brown sugar
  • 2 eggs
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1 1/2 teaspoons ground cinnamon
  • 3 cups Oatmeal & Company oats
    • Instructions:
    In a medium bowl, cream together butter, white sugar, and brown sugar. Beat in eggs one at a time, then stir in vanilla. Combine flour, baking soda, salt, and cinnamon; stir into the creamed mixture. Mix in oats. Cover, and chill dough for at least one hour. Preheat the oven to 375 degrees F (190 degrees C). Grease cookie sheets. Roll the dough into walnut sized balls, and place 2 inches apart on cookie sheets. Flatten each cookie with a large fork dipped in sugar. Bake for 8 to 10 minutes in preheated oven. Allow cookies to cool on baking sheet for 5 minutes before transferring to a wire rack to cool completely.
